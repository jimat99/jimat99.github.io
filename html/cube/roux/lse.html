LSE (Last Six Edges)

The goal of LSE (Last Six Edges) is to solve all the 6 edges remaining, which is 4 edges on the top layer (U layer) + 2 edges on the bottom layer (D layer).

The steps of LSE:

  • 4a. Edge Orientation (EO)
  • 4b. UL & UR Edges
  • 4c. 3-Cycle (5 cases)

Step 4a: Edge Orientation (EO)

The first step of LSE is edge orientation, which make all the remaining 6 edges to become oriented (white/yellow on top/bottom) as shown in the image below. In the first image, all 6 edges are misoriented. In the second image (top layer) and third image (bottom layer), all 6 edges are already oriented (all edges become white/yellow).

Notice that I used blue as the front layer.

Step 4b: UL & UR Edges

After the step 4a which make all the remaining 6 edges became oriented (white/yellow on top/bottom). The next step is to solve upper left (UL) edges and upper right (UR) edges as shown in the image below. In the first image, the UL & UR edges that we want to solve is placed in the front upper (FU) and left upper (LU), which is red yellow edge and orange yellow edge. After that, in the second image (right side red) and third image (left side orange), the UL and UR edges are already solved.

Notice that I used blue as the front layer.
