LSE (Last Six Edges)

The goal of LSE (Last Six Edges) is to solve all the 6 edges remaining, which is 4 edges on the top layer (U layer) + 2 edges on the bottom layer (D layer) as shown in the image below.

The steps of LSE:

  • 4a. Edge Orientation (EO)
  • 4b. UL & UR Edges
  • 4c. 3-Cycle (5 cases)

Step 4a: Edge Orientation

The first step of LSE is edge orientation, which make all the remaining 6 edges to become oriented (white/yellow on top/bottom) as shown in the image below.

Step 2: Corner Permutation (2 cases)

After the first step (corner orientation) which solved all the top 4 corners to become yellow facing on top.
